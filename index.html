I'm a Business Computer Information Systems student at the University of North Texas, wrapping up my degree in May 2026. My focus is on data analytics, solutions architecture, and the space where business problems meet technical solutions — basically, I like figuring out what the data is actually saying and turning that into something useful.

I had the chance to intern at Dell Technologies this past summer, where I got thrown into real client work fast. I was part of a cross-functional team building out AI/ML infrastructure proposals and presenting data protection solutions to clients. That experience pushed me to think less like a student and more like someone who has to defend a recommendation in front of decision-makers.

On campus, I run a Power BI dashboard that tracks loan data in real time, and I mentor over 100 students on personal finance. I really enjoy the mentoring side — especially working with students who have unpredictable income and helping them build a system that actually fits their life, not some textbook budget.

Outside of work, I'm interested in how analytics intersects with sports — which explains the NBA project. I'm also AWS-certified and always building on the cloud side of things. If any of this sounds like what your team is working on, let's talk.
